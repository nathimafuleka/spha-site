ISIHLOKO

A Poetry Short Film by Mbali Malimela

ISIHLOKO is a poetic short film written and performed by Zulu poet Mbali Malimela, known for her evocative storytelling rooted in African identity, memory, and emotional depth. The film explores how language, history, and emotion intertwine through the lens of a curious individual who stumbles upon a series of old love letters—letters that once passed between a distant couple.

What begins as a passive discovery quickly evolves into an emotional investigation. The individual, captivated by the intensity of the written words, begins to study the letters obsessively—searching for clues, meanings, and emotional patterns that unravel the layered state of the couple’s relationship.

Creative Approach

Creative Approach:

The narrative moves fluidly between two worlds: the present-day reader and their imagined interpretations of the couple’s story. Through visual metaphors and poetic narration, we are transported into a dreamlike reconstruction of what might have taken place—told entirely from the reader’s perspective.

Malimela’s poetry acts as both a guide and a pulse, breathing life into the silent intimacy of letters, and allowing viewers to feel the emotional weight of longing, miscommunication, and vulnerability that exists between two people trying to hold on through words.

Key Elements:
  • ★ Original Zulu poetry by Mbali Malimela
  • ★ Cinematic reenactments of imagined memories and relationships
  • ★ Use of natural lighting, slow pacing, and symbolic imagery
  • ★ A poetic voiceover that ties the past and present into one emotional arc

Outcome

Isihloko is currently making its way through the festival circuit, receiving praise for its unique blend of poetic narrative and visual storytelling. It is also part of a larger anthology series in development that aims to spotlight African poetic voices through film. The project highlights the emotional complexity of relationships and the power of language to preserve, distort, or reimagine memory.
