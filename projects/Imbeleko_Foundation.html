Imbeleko Foundation

Empowering Futures in the Valley of a Thousand Hills

Our ongoing visual storytelling collaboration with Imbeleko Foundation is rooted in purpose, place, and impact. Based in KwaNyuswa—deep in the heart of the Valley of a Thousand Hills—Imbeleko is a non-profit organisation founded by twin sisters, Dr. Seni Myeni-Offiah and Sbu Myeni. Both were raised in this very village by parents dedicated to health and community development, and after receiving full scholarships to study at Inkamana High School and later at the University of Cape Town, they returned with a mission: to invest in the potential of the children who come from the same hills that shaped them.

Creative Approach

Creative Approach:

Through video and photography, we document Imbeleko’s powerful work in education, youth development, healthcare, and leadership support. The storytelling is immersive and intimate—framed against the breathtaking landscapes of KwaNyuswa. Every frame is designed to reflect not only the beauty of the Valley, but also the strength and spirit of its people.

Our visual content captures:

  • Day-to-day educational and mentorship programs
  • Home visits and family outreach
  • Youth empowerment events and cultural experiences
  • Portraits and personal stories of the children and young adults supported by Imbeleko
Key Elements:
  • ★ Authentic and respectful visual storytelling rooted in real experiences
  • ★ Cinematic use of natural landscapes to complement the human stories
  • ★ Emotional interviews and on-location soundscapes
  • ★ A focus on legacy, identity, and community-driven impact

Outcome

The content produced has been used for fundraising, digital campaigns, stakeholder reports, and social media storytelling—amplifying the organisation’s reach both locally and internationally. Our work supports Imbeleko’s vision of nurturing talent within the Valley by reminding the world of the beauty, resilience, and brilliance found in rural South Africa.
